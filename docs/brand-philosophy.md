# Glass Cube — A Design Philosophy

**Glassbox AI Brand Identity · v2.0 · April 2026**

---

## I. The Central Tension

Every diagram of a neural network ever published shows the same lie: clean arrows, tidy boxes, the illusion that intelligence is something you can draw. Glass Cube refuses this comfortable fiction. It is a design language built on the tension between what is hidden and what is revealed — the black box made luminous, not by simplification, but by precise, unsparing illumination. The mark is a window cut into the dark, not decorated darkness.

## II. Form as Instrument

In Glass Cube, geometry is not decoration. it is measurement. The isometric hexagon is not a container; it is a frame for observation, the way a microscope slide holds a specimen in disciplined stillness so that something invisible becomes visible. The three face planes of the cube carry different levels of transparency — top face, right face, left face — each at precisely calibrated opacity. Nodes and connection weights drawn on the right face are not abstract shapes; they are the faithful residue of a computational event, drawn with the same care a physicist brings to a Feynman diagram. Every line earns its existence. Every curve follows the path information actually travels.

## III. Color as Temperature

The palette maps directly to the physics of the problem. Cyan (#00C8E8) is the primary signal: cool, precise, the wavelength of glass under light — transparent yet unmistakable. It is both the output signal and the internal computation simultaneously, a deliberate collapse of the distinction. The three accent tones — base cyan, deep (#009AB5), and light (#38D8F0) — represent the same signal at different intensities: full power, attenuated, and luminous trace. These are not brand colors in the marketing sense. They are temperature readings on a spectrum. A designer working in Glass Cube never picks a color for mood; they pick it for the *energy level* it carries.

## IV. Light, Glass, and Precision

The mark contains both opacity and transparency simultaneously — not as a stylistic gesture but as a philosophical commitment. Glass transmits light while imposing a frame. It is the ideal metaphor for mechanistic interpretability: the model is not opened up and simplified; it is made see-through while remaining complete. The isometric projection is critical. It shows three faces of the cube at once, which is exactly what mechanistic interpretability does: it exposes the internal structure of a system from multiple orthogonal angles without collapsing it into a single view. Luminosity is achieved through layered glow — the hex outline radiates soft concentric rings, the neural network nodes pulse with radial fills, the divider lines carry directional energy. The execution is painstaking: every gradient exactly calibrated, every glow radius precisely set, every node placement geometrically deliberate.

## V. Typography as Signal

Type in Glass Cube is architectural. Wordmarks are set in condensed geometric weight — Big Shoulders Display or Syne — tall, engineered letterforms that suggest precision manufacturing rather than personality. The "AI" sub-tag uses Jura at 300 weight with 0.45em tracking, as if reading an instrument panel. No decorative flourishes, no calligraphic warmth. Text exists to anchor, not to charm. The ratio of visual mark to written word mirrors the signal-to-noise ideal the company pursues in its science: the mark carries most of the meaning; the word confirms it.

## VI. The Neural Network on the Right Face

The three-layer network drawn on the right face of the cube is not decorative. It maps to the actual architecture used in circuit-level analysis: input layer (2 nodes), hidden layer (3 nodes), output layer (2 nodes). The bilinear projection onto the isometric face preserves the topology exactly. This is the product shown inside the product's own logo — a mark that only this company can legitimately use, because it references the actual science.
